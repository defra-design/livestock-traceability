The Animal Health and Welfare Review (AHWR) is a mandatory annual requirement. You must arrange for a vet to visit your holding to review the health and welfare of your livestock.

Use this service to record the results of your review and submit your data to Defra.

Before you start

You will need:

  • your vet’s Royal College of Veterinary Surgeons (RCVS) number
  • annual antibiotic usage data (mg/PCU) for each species on your holding (dairy, beef, sheep, or pigs)

For cattle keepers

  • total cases of clinical mastitis treated
  • total number of new lameness cases
  • total number of calves born alive
  • the biosecurity score provided by your vet during the visit

For sheep keepers

  • ewe wastage data (number of ewes died or culled)
  • scanning results (number of ewes mated versus those confirmed pregnant)
  • lambing and rearing data (number of lambs born alive versus those weaned)
  • anthelmintic (wormer) usage data
